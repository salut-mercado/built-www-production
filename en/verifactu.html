What is Verifactu?

Verifactu is the new billing control system implemented by the Spanish Tax Agency to prevent tax fraud. Simply put: your POS or management software must record every sale in an unalterable way and securely send the info to Hacienda.

If you're asking 'what is Verifactu', the short answer is: it's the law that changes how you issue tickets and invoices. From now on, your sales system must generate records with an electronic signature, consecutive numbering, and a time stamp so no one can modify them later.

It's not a program you download from Hacienda's website. It is a Verifactu regulation that your billing software must comply with to keep you protected during an inspection.

Key aspects of the Verifactu regulation and how it affects your billing

The Verifactu regulation establishes clear rules on how your billing must work. These are the three obligations you must know:

Unalterable record

Each ticket or invoice is saved with a timestamp and digital signature. It cannot be deleted or edited after being issued.

Consecutive numbering

Documents must follow a consecutive order without gaps or duplicates.

Sent to Hacienda

The information must be available to the Tax Agency in the format and timeframe required by law.
